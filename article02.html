BLUE HOUR

lkhmemories — Dec 16, 2025

The term “blue hour,” derived from the French l’heure bleue. It describes a brief phase of twilight, occurring either in the morning or the evening, most commonly around nautical twilight, when the Sun lies well below the horizon. At this time, the remaining ambient light takes on a distinctly blue tone, one that differs from the blue of a clear daytime sky.

The blue hour is especially treasured by photographers and is often considered part of the broader “magic hour.” It typically appears about 30 minutes after sunset, before darkness fully sets in. Physically, this effect arises as sunlight travels through the atmosphere: wavelengths in the 575–603 nm range are strongly absorbed by the ozone layer through Chappuis absorption, allowing shorter blue wavelengths to dominate.

Combined with atmospheric scattering, this process spreads blue light evenly across the sky, lending the landscape a serene, almost dreamlike quality.
